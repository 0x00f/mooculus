How else can I calculate area?

We've already done some area calculations, but it might surprise you to find out that we can  calculate area using horizontal rectangles. And if we can do it with both horizontal and vertical rectangles, we should think about  how to decide which sort of rectangle to use.

You can practice  area calculations on mooculus.

How can I calculate volume?

The basic idea for calculating volume is to  slice solid objects up into pieces, and integrate the volume of the pieces. The idea is that the volume formula for the pieces is much easier, so integration is promoting “easy” knowledge into the harder knowledge of volume of crazily shaped object.

With disks!

Volume calculations are discussed starting on  Page 230 of the Textbook, and there you can find some truly beautiful three-dimensional pictures. Please take a look! Once we have the basic idea, we can  calculate the volume of a sphere by slicing the sphere into thin disks.

With washers!

One nice source of examples are solids of revolution. If there's a cavity in such, we might try  slicing the object into washers to compute its volume.

With shells!

The pieces can be more complicated than just cross sections. For instance, we can cut an object  into thin shells, providing a nice way to  calculate the volume of a sphere with a hole drilled into it.

You can practice  volume calculations on mooculus.

How can I calculate length?

Length is  a bit more subtle than one might think, but we have a definition in terms of an integral. We can use that definition to compute  the length of an arc along the graph of $$y^2 = x^3$$.

You can practice  arc length calculations on mooculus.
