Continuity? What does “continuous” mean?

Continuity makes precise the idea that small changes in the input don't affect the output too much. To learn the definition, watch  an introduction to continuity and then  try some practice problems. You can find more practice problems in  Section §1.6 of the Textbook.

Many of the functions we think about are continuous: for instance, polynomials are continuous. We'll be considering continuity more in the weeks to come.

Once we know the basics of continuity, we study the intermediate value theorem. Learn the statement by watching  What is the intermediate value theorem? Then we apply the theorem to two problems:  approximating $$\sqrt{2}$$ and  finding fixed points. You can practice using the theorem yourself by  finding roots using the bisection method. Computers use this bisection algorithm to approximate roots numerically.

Infinity? How can I work with that?

People have thought about infinity for thousands of years; limits provide one way to make such ponderings precise. To see how limits handle infinity, we observe that “infinity” may play a role in the output or in the input: when the output of a function is as large as we like, we speak about  $$\displaystyle\lim f(x) = \infty$$; when the input is large, we may speak about  $$\displaystyle\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$$. Pictures of these two situations can be found in  Section §1.4 and Section §1.5 of the Textbook.

Be sure to practice working with infinity by working through  the exercises on mooculus.

Sometimes people are tempted to do calculations with $$\infty$$ as if it were a number, but  $$\infty$$ is not a number so you cannot expect “calculations” like $$\displaystyle\frac{\infty}{\infty}$$ to be meaningful. It's fun to think about infinity, so you may enjoy debating some  paradoxes involving $$\infty - \infty$$ in the forum.

Slope?

Slope measures the “steepness” of a line. Because we'll be making use of slope next week when we start studying derivatives, it's important to review slope, which we do by considering a couple real world problems:  the slope of a staircase and  how fast water drips from a faucet.
